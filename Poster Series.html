Poster Series

For the poster series i designed 3 products taking inspiration form 3 iconic art movements- Rococo. Post-Impressionism and Neoclassicism.

  



Playing cards - Rococo Period



The first poster displays playing cards designed by taking inspiration from the Rococo Movement. The period is known for its heavy ornamentation and pastel colour palette. It is considered rather whimsical. I particularly took inspiration from the architecture and furniture of the time for designing the cards.

 



Concert ticket - Post Impressionism

The second product is designed taking inspiration form post Impressionism. It was predominantly a French art movement that broke free from the naturalism of Impressionism. I Focused on the style of Henri Matisse. He moved further away from the need for realistic representation, experimenting with creating a sense of balance and pleasure in his paintings. In his artworks, the human figure are central and manipulated. I chose to design concert tickets. The Band i chose is Les Six, which itself is a post impressinist band. The Design of the Ticket is inspired by their most famous composition “Les mariés de la tour Eiffel” (The Wedding Party on the Eiffel Tower).

 




Matchbox - Neoclassicism

I designed the match taking inspiration for the Neoclassicism Movement. Neoclassical architecture is defined as the “revival of classical architecture during the 18th and early 19th centuries.” It is a distinct architectural style that references elements of classical antiquity—mostly ancient Greek and Roman architecture—that flourished throughout Western countries, particularly in Europe and the United States. For my design, I chose the Lincoln Memorial built from 1914 to 1922. It is a Classical block building. Block buildings typically have exterior columns and flat roofs. These neoclassical structures are also designed around a square or rectangular floor plan. For the colour palette I took inspiration from the paintings of the time. I noticed the prominent use of reds, blues, greens and yellows. I wanted the design to have a vintage feel typical to that of a matchbox. For the layout I kept it simple and harmonious, which are one of the key elements of the architectural style.
